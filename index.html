The problem

You know the feeling.

Some months $8,000 lands in your account and you feel rich. The next month $1,200 trickles in and you're terrified.

You've tried "just saving more." You've tried spreadsheets that you abandoned by week two. You've downloaded QuickBooks, taken one look at the chart of accounts, and closed the tab.

The problem isn't you. The problem is that every personal finance tool ever built assumes you get paid the same amount on the 1st and the 15th. You don't. So you're stuck doing freelance finance on hard mode, paying yourself whatever's left over after the panic subsides.

The solution

There's a number that fixes this.

It's the largest amount you can pay yourself every single month without running out during a slow stretch. Big enough to actually live on. Small enough that a quiet month doesn't blow up your life.

Smooth Paycheck calculates that number. In about five minutes. Using the income you've already earned.

That's it. That's the whole product.

Questions

Things people ask first.

Do I need to connect my bank account?
No. You type in (or paste) your monthly income totals. Nothing connects to your bank, and your data stays in your browser.
Is this accounting software?
No, and that's the point. Accounting software tracks every transaction. Smooth Paycheck answers one question: what can I pay myself this month?
What if my income is really unpredictable?
Then you need this more than anyone. The Stability Score and buffer target are specifically built for high-volatility income. That's the whole reason it exists.
Does this work outside the US?
Yes. Tax rate is a slider you set yourself, and the currency adjusts. Works for freelancers in any country.
Will my data be safe?
Your numbers never leave your device. There's no account, no login, no server storing your income. Just a tool that runs in your browser.
